## Why Wonder to Build exists

Where I grew up in Qalladizah, Kurdistan, being "good with technology" meant you could fix a phone or reset a password. If you could reproduce an Arduino project from YouTube, people called you an inventor. The praise felt good — but something was wrong.

Nobody was asking: *did you actually design this? Do you understand why it works? Could you build something different on your own?*

That gap — between copying and creating — is what Wonder to Build was built to close.

## What we're building

We're creating a system that takes students from their first circuit all the way to deploying machine learning models on microcontrollers. But the technical skills are only half the story. We also teach:

- **Intellectual property literacy** — understanding what originality means
- **Documentation habits** — proving your work is yours with timestamps and version control
- **AI ethics** — using AI as a tool, not a shortcut

## What comes next

Our first Spark Workshops launch in Kurdistan schools in July 2026. If you're a student, an educator, a donor, or just someone who believes that every young person deserves the chance to build something real — we'd love to hear from you.

This is just the beginning.
