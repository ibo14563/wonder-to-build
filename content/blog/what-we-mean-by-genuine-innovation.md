## The Facebook hacker problem

There's a joke in many communities: someone who can reset a Facebook password is called a "hacker." Someone who follows a YouTube tutorial and builds the same circuit as ten thousand other people is called an "inventor."

The joke is funny because it's true. And it points to a serious problem.

## Why this matters now

With AI tools like ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot, the gap between imitation and creation is getting wider. A student can now generate an entire project — code, documentation, even a presentation — without understanding any of it. That's not innovation. That's a new kind of copying.

## How we teach the difference

At Wonder to Build, every student learns to:

1. **Start with a question** — not a tutorial
2. **Document from day one** — Git commits with timestamps
3. **Explain their work** — if you can't explain it, you didn't build it
4. **Use AI transparently** — as a tool, with attribution, not as a ghost author

The goal isn't to ban AI or reject tutorials. It's to make sure students are the ones doing the thinking.

## The proof is in the portfolio

Every Wonder to Build graduate has a GitHub Pages portfolio with timestamped, version-controlled, publicly verifiable work. That's not just a credential — it's a commitment to authenticity.
